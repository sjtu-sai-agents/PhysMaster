# Problem

Consider the one-dimensional double-well potential
\[
V(x) = \frac{\lambda}{4}(x^2 - a^2)^2
\]
with \( \lambda > 0 \). The system has two degenerate classical minima at \( x = \pm a \).

---

## Task

Using semiclassical (Euclidean path integral) methods, derive an approximation for the energy splitting \( \Delta E \) between the ground state and first excited state due to quantum tunneling.

Your analysis should include the following components:

1. **Instanton Solution**  
   Find the classical Euclidean solution \( x(\tau) \) that interpolates between the two minima \( -a \to +a \).  
   Express the solution in closed form.

2. **Instanton Action**  
   Compute the Euclidean action \( S_{\text{inst}} \) associated with this trajectory.

3. **Dilute Instanton Gas Approximation**  
   Explain how multi-instanton configurations contribute and why they can be treated as a dilute gas.  
   Derive how repeated tunneling events modify the partition function.

4. **Energy Splitting**  
   Show that the ground-state energy splitting behaves as
   \[
   \Delta E \sim A \, e^{-S_{\text{inst}}/\hbar}
   \]
   and explain the origin of the prefactor \( A \) at a qualitative level (no need for full determinant calculation, but outline the structure).

---

## Goal

Provide a logically consistent derivation connecting:

- classical Euclidean solutions,
- semiclassical saddle-point approximation,
- multi-instanton contributions,
- and the emergence of energy splitting.

---

## Difficulty

Advanced (borderline research-level; requires familiarity with path integrals and semiclassical methods)
