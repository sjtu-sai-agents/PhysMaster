# Problem: Quantum Anharmonic Oscillator

Consider a one-dimensional quantum particle of mass \( m \) in the potential
\[
V(x) = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 x^2 + \lambda x^4
\]
where \( \lambda > 0 \).

---

## Tasks

### (1) First-Order Perturbation

Using time-independent perturbation theory, compute the first-order correction to the ground state energy due to the \( \lambda x^4 \) term.

You may use:
\[
\langle 0 | x^4 | 0 \rangle = \frac{3}{4} \left( \frac{\hbar}{m\omega} \right)^2
\]

---

### (2) Asymptotic Behavior

Discuss the behavior of the perturbation series at high orders:

- Does the series converge?
- If not, explain why it is still useful as an asymptotic expansion.

---

### (3) Numerical Estimation

Set \( m = \omega = \hbar = 1 \), and \( \lambda = 0.1 \).

- Use a numerical method (e.g., finite difference or matrix diagonalization) to estimate the ground state energy.
- Compare your result with the first-order perturbation result.

---

### (4) Physical Interpretation

Explain qualitatively:

- How the \( x^4 \) term affects the energy level spacing.
- What happens in the strong coupling limit \( \lambda \to \infty \).

---

### (5) Bonus

Consider the Euclidean path integral:
\[
Z = \int \mathcal{D}x(t) \, e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar} S[x]}
\]
with
\[
S[x] = \int dt \left( \frac{1}{2} m \dot{x}^2 + \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 x^2 + \lambda x^4 \right)
\]

Discuss whether saddle-point methods apply and whether nontrivial classical solutions (e.g., instanton-like configurations) may exist.
